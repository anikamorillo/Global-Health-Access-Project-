My Story

Hi! I’m Anika Morillo, a high school student passionate about healthcare and global health equity. I started the Global Health Access Project (GHAP) to explore how healthcare access differs around the world, learn from frontline healthcare professionals, and share those insights with others. Through this project, I hope to raise awareness about global disparities and inspire solutions that improve access for all.

Why This Matters

Growing up, I became interested in medicine and healthcare systems. I realized that access to quality healthcare is unequal, depending on where someone lives or their resources. GHAP allows me to combine my research interests with real-world impact, learning from professionals while sharing knowledge globally.
